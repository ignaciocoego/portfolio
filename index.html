02 / ABOUT

Ignacio Coego
// Who I Am

I'm Ignacio, a 4th-year Video Game Development student at Universidad Argentina de la Empresa (UADE). I've spent the last four years building games — between university projects, personal prototypes and collaborative work as part of Tango Byte, an independent studio I co-founded with fellow developers.

My strongest areas are Unity development in C#, gameplay systems, and level design and environmental lighting. I enjoy owning features end-to-end: from prototyping a trigger system to iterating on a map's atmosphere until the space feels alive. I care about craft, iteration and making things that feel good to play.

Outside of gamedev, my background in customer service, sales and team management has shaped me into a clear communicator who works well under pressure. I'm looking to contribute to real projects, learn from experienced teams, and grow as a developer and designer.

“What if the horror game was actively listening to you?”

A first-person psychological horror game where your real microphone becomes the main input — and the main threat. You play as Alex, an audio engineer called back to a defunct 1980s radio station after a ghostly transmission from his missing brother.

Trapped in a decaying broadcast facility built over a secret research complex, players navigate by sound, solve audio-driven puzzles and speak directly into the darkness while a signal-bound Entity hunts them through the static. No weapons. Only your voice, your tools, and the silence you choose to keep.

Built in Unity with a focus on hyperrealistic lighting, atmospheric level design and mic-driven gameplay systems.

// My Role — Systems Programming
  • Full trigger system architecture across the entire facility
  • Elevator mechanics and state management
  • Map transitions and scene flow
  • Dynamic light animations and behaviour states
  • Power-cut event and generator puzzle system
  • Scripted appearances of the scientist bodies
// My Role — Lighting & Level Design
  • Complete lighting and atmosphere of the facility in Unity
  • Light probes, baked GI and advanced light configurations
  • Hyperrealistic analog 1980s broadcast aesthetic
  • Co-designed the level layout of the Research Chambers (team of 3)
  • Environmental storytelling through props and lighting
  • Iteration based on playtesting and team feedback

“Defend your temple. Become a god.”

A virtual reality wave-defense game where players adopt god-like abilities to protect their last standing temple from waves of mythological enemies. Players can build and upgrade human defenses using mystical coins and discover unique interactions available only to a divine being.

A fully playable VR experience built in Unity, with a focus on intuitive motion controls, satisfying combat and a strong sense of power fantasy.
